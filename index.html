SHELTER OF LOVE

SHELTER OF LOVE was founded in 2024 with a mission to provide shelter, care, and support to those most in need in our community. We believe in love, dignity, and hope for everyone, and through our various programs, we aim to make a meaningful difference.

What We Do

Emergency Housing

Our shelter provides housing to individuals and families in urgent need. We offer a safe space, meals, and basic necessities to help them get back on their feet.

Food Distribution

We distribute meals and food packages to the homeless and those struggling with food insecurity. Our goal is to ensure no one goes hungry.

SHELTER OF LOVE FOUNDATION - Framework

Summary

Shelter of Love is an organisation based in Lugari constituency, Kakamega County. Founded by Kenneth Wawire in 2024, it deeply targets the less fortunate in our society, with its focus in Lugari sub-county and other sub-counties and counties.

Mission

To alleviate poverty and suffering in our community through targeted assistance and empowerment initiatives.

Vision

A society where every individual has access to basic necessities and opportunities for a dignified life.

Aims

  • To eradicate poverty in our society
  • Provide shelter
  • Provide food and education
  • Provide clothes
  • Mental health awareness
  • Menstrual cycle hygiene

Achievements/Mission Accomplished

  • 18th March 2024: Visited Maturu Primary Special School, providing food, pads, toiletries, fruits, and adult diapers for 12 boarders out of 201 kids (100 girls and 101 boys).
  • 10th May 2024: Assisted a family in Mabuye area, Lwandeti ward, with food, clothes, toiletries, prayers, and built a temporary shelter for the mother caring for two disabled boys.
  • 18th May 2024: Supported a father with two children in Magut area, Uasin Gishu County. We provided beddings, food, clothes, toiletries, and paid school fees for the boys at Lower Kipkaren Primary School.
  • 20th June 2024: Partnered with Rotary Club of Bungoma and Daisy Nyongesa Foundation to conduct a pad drive at Mawa Primary School, donating 400 pads to 200 school-going girls.
